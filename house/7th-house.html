The 7th House: The House of Partnerships

By Hermes Astrology · January 05, 2026

In the astrological wheel, the 7th House stands directly opposite the 1st House—the western horizon where the sun sets, symbolizing completion, union, and the movement from self to other. Known traditionally as the "House of Marriage" or "Descendant House," this angular house governs all one-to-one relationships, partnerships, contracts, and the qualities we seek in others.

The 7th House corresponds to Libra energy and the cardinal air element. It represents our need for balance, harmony, and connection with others. Where the 1st House declares "I AM," the 7th House asks "WHO ARE WE?"—marking the transition from individual identity to relational consciousness.

The Descendant: Gateway to Relationships

The Descendant is the zodiac sign occupying the 7th House cusp at your moment of birth. This point reveals what we attract in partners and how we approach relationships:

  • Aries Descendant: Attracts independent, assertive partners; seeks excitement in relationships
  • Taurus Descendant: Attracts stable, reliable partners; values security and consistency
  • Gemini Descendant: Attracts communicative, versatile partners; needs mental stimulation
  • Cancer Descendant: Attracts nurturing, protective partners; seeks emotional security
  • Leo Descendant: Attracts confident, creative partners; wants admiration and warmth
  • Virgo Descendant: Attracts practical, helpful partners; values service and improvement
  • Libra Descendant: Attracts diplomatic, artistic partners; seeks balance and harmony
  • Scorpio Descendant: Attracts intense, transformative partners; desires deep bonding
  • Sagittarius Descendant: Attracts adventurous, philosophical partners; needs freedom and growth
  • Capricorn Descendant: Attracts responsible, ambitious partners; values structure and commitment
  • Aquarius Descendant: Attracts unique, independent partners; seeks intellectual connection
  • Pisces Descendant: Attracts compassionate, spiritual partners; desires soulful merging

Your Descendant represents both what you lack within yourself and seek in others, as well as the qualities you project onto partners. It's the mirror that shows you what you need to integrate for wholeness.

Planets in the 7th House

Planets in the 7th House significantly color your approach to relationships and the type of partners you attract. These energies manifest prominently in your interactions with others:

  • Sun in 7th House: Identity through relationships, needs partnership for self-expression, may attract dominant partners
  • Moon in 7th House: Emotional needs met through relationships, nurturing partners, public emotionality
  • Mercury in 7th House: Communicative partnerships, intellectual connection essential, may attract younger partners
  • Venus in 7th House: Harmonious relationships, artistic partners, strong desire for partnership, natural diplomacy
  • Mars in 7th House: Passionate relationships, competitive partners, needs independence within union
  • Jupiter in 7th House: Expansive relationships, fortunate partnerships, may attract foreign or philosophical partners
  • Saturn in 7th House: Serious commitments, older or responsible partners, delays in marriage, relationship lessons
  • Uranus in 7th House: Unconventional relationships, sudden partnerships, needs freedom, may attract unique individuals
  • Neptune in 7th House: Idealistic relationships, spiritual connections, confusion in partnerships, artistically inclined partners
  • Pluto in 7th House: Transformative relationships, intense partnerships, power dynamics, rebirth through others

Multiple planets in the 7th House indicate that relationships are a primary life focus, with complex dynamics and multiple significant partnerships throughout life.

Types of Relationships Governed by the 7th House

While commonly called the "Marriage House," the 7th House governs all significant one-to-one connections:

  • Romantic partnerships and marriage
  • Business partnerships and collaborations
  • Close friendships with equal commitment
  • Client relationships (in service professions)
  • Legal adversaries and open enemies
  • Contractual agreements of all kinds
  • Diplomatic relations and negotiations

The 7th House essentially covers anyone with whom you have a formal or informal agreement, explicit or implicit.

The Shadow Side: Projection and the "Other"

Psychologically, the 7th House represents what Carl Jung called "the shadow"—qualities we disown in ourselves but recognize (and sometimes criticize) in others:

  1. Projection: Seeing in partners what we refuse to acknowledge in ourselves
  2. Mirroring: Partners reflecting back our unintegrated aspects
  3. Dependency: Seeking in others what we won't develop independently
  4. Balance: Learning through relationship what we lack as individuals

Challenging aspects to the Descendant or difficult planets in the 7th House often indicate repeated relationship patterns that force us to confront and integrate these disowned parts of ourselves.

Empty 7th House vs. Occupied 7th House

An empty 7th House (no planets) suggests:

  • Relationships may not be the primary life focus
  • More objectivity in partnerships (less personal planet interference)
  • Look to the Descendant ruler's placement for relationship patterns
  • May have fewer but more significant relationships

An occupied 7th House, especially with personal planets, indicates:

  • Relationships are central to personal development
  • Strong needs and expectations in partnerships
  • Visible planetary energies in relationship style
  • Potential for codependency if unbalanced

The 7th House in Different Astrological Systems

Various traditions interpret the 7th House with different emphases:

  • Traditional/Hellenistic: Focus on marriage, business partnerships, and open enemies; the house of "the other"
  • Psychological Astrology: Emphasizes projection, mirroring, and relationship as path to wholeness
  • Evolutionary Astrology: Views the 7th House as karmic relationships and soul contracts
  • Vedic Astrology (Jyotish): Calls it the "Kalatra Bhava" — house of spouse and partnerships, crucial for marital happiness

Transits and Progressions Through the 7th House

When planets transit your 7th House, you experience:

  • Increased focus on relationships and partnerships
  • Meeting significant others or deepening existing bonds
  • Legal matters or contracts coming to the forefront
  • Confrontations with "the other" (partners or adversaries)

Saturn transiting the 7th House (approximately 2.5 years) often brings relationship tests, commitments, or delays that ultimately lead to more mature partnerships. Jupiter transiting the 7th House expands relationship opportunities and brings beneficial connections. Pluto transiting the 7th House can trigger profound relationship transformations and power dynamics.

Progressed Moon through the 7th House marks a 2.5-year period of emotional focus on relationships and changing partnership needs.

Integrating the 7th House with the Whole Chart

The 7th House must be understood in relation to other houses:

  • Opposite the 1st House: Balance between self (1st) and other (7th) is the central axis
  • Square the 4th/10th Houses: Tension between partnership needs and family/career demands
  • Trine the 3rd/11th Houses: Natural relationship expression through communication and social networks
  • The Descendant Ruler: Where this planet is placed shows how you approach relationships

For example, someone with Libra on the 7th House cusp (Descendant) whose ruler Venus is in the 10th House (Capricorn) might seek partners who enhance their public reputation or meet through career connections.

The Evolution of 7th House Consciousness

The 7th House journey involves moving from dependency to interdependence:

  1. Projection Stage: Seeking in others what we lack
  2. Disillusionment Stage: Discovering partners are separate individuals
  3. Integration Stage: Taking back projections and developing those qualities
  4. Conscious Partnership: Relating from wholeness rather than need

Business and Legal Implications

Beyond personal relationships, the 7th House governs:

  • Contracts and agreements of all types
  • Business partnerships and collaborations
  • Legal matters, especially lawsuits and negotiations
  • Diplomacy and public relations
  • Client-based businesses (consulting, therapy, law)

A strong 7th House (well-aspected Venus, Jupiter, or benefic aspects) often indicates skill in negotiation, partnership success, or legal acumen.

The Spiritual Dimension of the 7th House

Ultimately, the 7th House teaches that relationship is the great mirror. Every partnership—romantic, business, or adversarial—shows us aspects of ourselves we cannot see alone. Through the dance of self and other, we discover that true relationship begins not when we find the perfect partner, but when we become the partner we seek.

The house that asks "WHO ARE WE?" contains the secret that in knowing others, we come to know ourselves more deeply. By consciously working with 7th House energy, we transform relationships from arenas of need and projection to sacred spaces of mutual growth, where two wholes meet to create something greater than either could alone.
